# ADR-003: Most-Recent-Exact-Score Tiebreaker

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-27

## Context

When members are tied on total points, the current API breaks ties by `joined_at` ascending — earliest joiner ranks higher. The PRD requires a tiebreaker that rewards late-tournament performance instead: the member who scored an exact prediction (correct home **and** away goals) on the most recent match should rank higher. This makes the ranking dynamic deep into the tournament rather than frozen by signup order. The edge case of tied members with zero exact scores must also be deterministic.

## Decision

Replace the `joined_at` tiebreaker with a three-level comparator applied within each points tier:

1. **Has any exact score** (desc) — members with at least one exact score outrank members with none.
2. **Most recent exact-score match date** (desc) — among members with exact scores, the one whose latest exact prediction lands on the match with the later `match_date` (kickoff) ranks higher.
3. **Full name** (A→Z, locale-aware) — final deterministic fallback, also covers two members who scored their most recent exact in the same match and all-zero pre-tournament leagues.

The comparator lives in the shared `lib/ranking.ts` helper so both the panel and ranking endpoints produce identical orderings. "Most recent exact" is derived by tracking, per member, the maximum `match_date` across that member's exact-score predictions on finished matches.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Keep `joined_at` tiebreaker

- **Description**: Retain the existing earliest-joiner-wins rule.
- **Pros**: No change; already implemented and tested.
- **Cons**: Static and arbitrary — rewards signup time, not skill; ranking never shifts on ties as the tournament progresses.
- **Why rejected**: Directly contradicts the PRD goal of late-tournament relevance.

### Alternative 2: No-exact members interleave alphabetically with exact members

- **Description**: Skip the "has any exact" level; sort the whole tied group by most-recent-exact date (nulls treated as equal) then by name, allowing a no-exact member to sort above an exact one by name.
- **Pros**: Slightly simpler comparator.
- **Cons**: A member who never scored an exact could outrank one who did, undermining the "reward exact predictions" intent.
- **Why rejected**: Violates the principle that any exact score should beat none on a tie.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Ranking order shifts meaningfully as matches finish, increasing late-tournament engagement.
- Fully deterministic and verifiable: every tie resolves to a single stable order.
- Pre-tournament all-zero leagues fall through to alphabetical order naturally (no member has an exact), satisfying the empty-state requirement.

### Negative

- The ranking computation must additionally fetch and carry `match_date` for finished matches (the panel endpoint does not select it today).

### Risks

- Locale-sensitive name sorting could differ across environments. Mitigation: use a fixed `localeCompare` with `'pt-BR'` and document it; cover with a unit test.

## Implementation Notes

- Per member, accumulate `mostRecentExactDate = max(match_date)` over predictions where `predicted_home_score === home_score && predicted_away_score === away_score`.
- Comparator order: `points` desc → `hasExact` desc → `mostRecentExactDate` desc → `fullName.localeCompare(otherName, 'pt-BR')` asc.
- Members with a null `full_name` sort after named members at the final level (treat null as an empty string is acceptable since name is effectively always present).
