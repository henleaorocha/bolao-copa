# ADR-001: Dedicated Bet Screen + Full Palpites Page as Primary Betting Experience

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-25

## Context

The betting feature needs to let users see upcoming matches and submit score predictions. The design references provide two distinct surfaces: a match detail screen for placing an individual bet (Image #12 — full-screen hero with team flags, score inputs, scoring breakdown, and distribution chart) and a full Palpites list page (Image #13 — all 72 group-stage matches with date/group filters). The core decision was how these two surfaces relate to each other and to the league panel.

Three viable approaches were considered: a dedicated bet screen accessible from both the panel and the Palpites list; inline betting within the panel cards; and a single surface (Palpites list) as the only bet entry point.

## Decision

Use Approach A: a dedicated full-screen bet page as the primary interaction for placing a prediction on a single match. The league panel shows the next 4 upcoming matches; clicking any match navigates to the dedicated bet screen. The "Ver Todos" CTA on the panel navigates to the full Palpites page listing all 72 group-stage matches. Each match row on the Palpites page is also clickable and leads to the same bet screen. This matches the design references exactly.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative B: Inline Bets in the Panel

- **Description**: Score inputs appear directly on the 4 match cards in the league panel. No navigation required to place a bet on upcoming games.
- **Pros**: Fewer taps to save a prediction; power users can bet without leaving the panel.
- **Cons**: Clutters the panel layout; no room for the scoring breakdown or distribution chart; diverges from the design reference.
- **Why rejected**: The scoring explanation ("Quanto vale acertar") and the bet distribution chart are key UX elements that require their own space. The design reference clearly shows a dedicated screen.

### Alternative C: Palpites Page as the Only Bet Surface

- **Description**: No separate bet detail page. Clicking a match in the panel navigates to the Palpites page scrolled to that match. Bets are placed inline in the list rows.
- **Pros**: Single surface to maintain; simpler navigation model.
- **Cons**: Dense on mobile; distribution chart and scoring breakdown have no good place to live; deviates from design references.
- **Why rejected**: The bet detail experience is rich enough (two information panels below the hero) to warrant its own screen. Inline list editing would not accommodate this content.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Exact implementation of the approved design references — no design rework needed.
- Clear, focused experience: users know exactly where they are when placing a bet.
- Scoring breakdown and distribution chart have dedicated space, reinforcing league engagement.

### Negative

- More navigation steps from panel to saving a bet (panel → bet screen → save).
- Two entry points to the same bet screen (panel and Palpites list) must stay in sync.

### Risks

- Navigation depth may feel excessive on mobile. Mitigated by a prominent "Voltar" button and the unsaved-changes modal that prevents accidental data loss.
