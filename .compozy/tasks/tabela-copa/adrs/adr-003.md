# ADR-003: Supabase pg_cron as the Hourly Sync Trigger

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-26

## Context

ADR-001 established that match scores are refreshed by a background sync job that calls
API-Football at most once per hour, keeping the page load path external-API-free. The
sync logic already exists as `POST /api/admin/sync-matches` (authenticated with
`SUPABASE_SERVICE_ROLE_KEY`). What remained undecided was the **trigger**: what invokes
that route every hour.

A hard project constraint is cost: the trigger must not introduce any new billable
surface or risk crossing a free-tier limit into paid usage. Two limits are in play:

- **API-Football free tier**: ~100 requests/day. An hourly sync is 24 requests/day —
  well within budget. A sub-hourly cadence (e.g. every 5 min = 288/day) would exceed it.
- **Hosting/scheduler tiers**: vary by provider (see alternatives).

## Decision

Trigger the sync with **Supabase `pg_cron`** (scheduling) plus **`pg_net`** (outbound
HTTP), both running inside the Postgres instance the project already uses. A scheduled
job fires hourly (`0 * * * *`) and issues an authenticated `http_post` to the existing
`/api/admin/sync-matches` route. The cadence is fixed at hourly, which keeps
API-Football usage at 24 requests/day.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Vercel Cron

- **Description**: A `crons` entry in `vercel.json` hits the sync route on a schedule.
- **Pros**: Native to a Next.js/Vercel deploy; versioned in the repo; no extra infra.
- **Cons**: On the **Hobby (free) plan, cron jobs run at most once per day** — hourly
  cadence requires the **Pro plan (~US$20/month)**.
- **Why rejected**: Directly violates the cost constraint; hourly scheduling would force
  a paid plan upgrade.

### Alternative 2: External cron (GitHub Actions)

- **Description**: A scheduled GitHub Actions workflow `curl`s the sync endpoint hourly.
- **Pros**: Hosting-agnostic; runs are visible in CI logs; free for public repos.
- **Cons**: Consumes the Actions minutes budget (~720 of 2,000 free minutes/month on a
  private repo); schedule jitter; lives outside the app runtime as a separate moving part.
- **Why rejected**: Consumes a metered budget that could approach its limit, and adds an
  out-of-repo dependency, when a zero-cost in-database option exists.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Zero new billing surface: `pg_cron` and `pg_net` are available on the Supabase free
  tier and run inside the database already provisioned.
- Provider-agnostic — independent of where the Next.js app is hosted.
- The hourly job keeps a free-tier Supabase project active (it pauses after ~7 days idle).
- Reuses the existing `/api/admin/sync-matches` route unchanged.

### Negative

- Requires enabling the `pg_cron` and `pg_net` extensions once (via migration).
- Scheduling lives in the database rather than a repo config file, so it is slightly
  less discoverable than `vercel.json`.
- `pg_net` HTTP calls are fire-and-forget, giving weaker delivery observability.

### Risks

- A silently failing job leaves standings up to 1h stale (already accepted in ADR-001).
  Mitigation: monitor the most recent successful sync; degrade gracefully to last known
  scores.

## Implementation Notes

- Add a migration enabling `pg_cron` and `pg_net` and registering the hourly job.
- Store the target app URL and the service-role authorization for the `http_post` call
  via Supabase config/secrets rather than hardcoding them in the migration.
- Keep the cron expression at `0 * * * *`; do not lower the interval, to stay under the
  API-Football free-tier daily cap.
